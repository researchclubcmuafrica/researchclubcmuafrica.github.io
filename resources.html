Research Club Workshop - LATEX: A Mini Tutorial

October 31, 2024

On 31st of October 2024, The Research Club hosted Prof. Edwin Mugume on getting started with LaTeX, an academic writing tool for researchers and scientists. He did an expository coverage of how CMU-Africa students can leverage Overleaf to write well formatted research papers and technical documents taking us through a live workshop with the participants. It was a very insightful learning session as students get to practicalize and use the knowledge in writing their various assignment reports and other documents.

Spring 2025 Activities Recap

The Research Club successfully organized and supported the following events in Spring 2025

CMU-Africa Students Research Showcase

May 6, 2025

To mark the end of a successful semester, the CMU-Africa Research Club hosted the Student Research Showcase, the first of its kind on our campus, focusing on course-based research! This event highlighted innovative, research conducted by students during the Spring 2025 semester. A few students gave live research presentations, receiving thoughtful feedback from faculty, guest researchers, and peers in attendance. We also featured a digital poster session, where students took turns presenting their work and engaging with attendees, a dynamic, interactive way to share their ideas and learn from each other. While this initiative spotlighted course-based projects, it's also worth noting that students involved in independent research and MSIT Research Project participated in a separate session with posters and short presentations to showcase their work. At Carnegie Mellon University Africa, we are committed to raising the next generation of African tech leaders and research is one of our most powerful tools in that mission.

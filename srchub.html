The CMU-Africa Research Club proudly introduces the Student Research Collaboration Hub (SRC Hub) — an initiative designed to boost student research output and foster meaningful collaborations across all experience levels.

The SRC Hub provides a central platform where students with innovative research ideas — from coursework, capstone projects, or independent exploration — can connect with peers and faculty to turn their ideas into publishable, high-quality research contributions.

Objectives

The SRC Hub aims to:

🧩

Increase student research output at CMU-Africa by promoting collaboration and mentorship.

📚

Facilitate publications in top-tier conferences and journals across engineering, AI, and interdisciplinary fields.

🌍

Foster a student–faculty research culture that supports long-term impact, knowledge exchange, and mentorship.

Initiatives

1 Student Project Advertisement

Students can share their individual or group research projects through the SRC Hub. Each primary researcher submits a proposal including:

  • Problem statement and research gap
  • Background and related work
  • Proposed methodology and approach
  • Required skills (e.g., coding, data analysis, writing)
  • Proposed timeline and milestones
  • Expected outcomes (e.g., publication, prototype, dataset)

A collaboration advert will accompany each proposal so peers can join projects based on interest and skill.

🔸 Important: Every project must have a faculty supervisor to provide guidance, feedback, and mentorship.

2 Research Assistant Collaboration

Research Assistants (RAs) currently working on faculty-led projects can also post opportunities on the SRC Hub.

RAs submit:

  • A brief description of the ongoing project
  • Required skills and expected contributions from collaborators
  • Approval from the supervising professor

This initiative enables hands-on collaboration under structured mentorship, allowing peers to contribute meaningfully while gaining valuable research experience.
